Chapter One: Understanding Nordic and Scandinavian

In the Nordic Countries, there are two entities; The Scandinavian Countries, and the Other Nordic Countries. All of the Nordic Countries together are Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Finland, and Iceland.


All five countries are Nordic, but three of them are Scandinavian while the other two aren't; The Scandinavian countries are Sweden, Denmark, and Norway, and the other Nordic countries are Finland and Iceland. In order to undertstand which Nordic countries are Scandinavian and which ones are not, we need to look at their history and georgraphy.


The term "Nordic" basically means countries that were colonoized, controlled by, or influenced by the Scandinavian countries.

Map of Nordic Countries; The countries colored in red are the Scandinavian Countries, while the countries shaded in orange are the other Nordic Countries (All countries are the Nordic Countries)




The countries that are Scandinavian have the following traits; they are located along the Scandinavian Peninsula and Denmark, their languages are descended from Old Norse and Germanic languages, the ancient societies of Vikings came from their lands, and the were responsible for colonization and influence of the other two Nordic Countries.


The Scandinavian countries are as followed:


Sweden is the largest country by population and land area, and It takes up the eastern half of the Scandinavian Peninsula.


Denmark is the smallest in land area but second in populaton, and it takes up the lands of Jutland and neighboring islands, all south of the main Scandinavian Peninsula (even though Denmark is not on the Scandinavian Peninsula and it is attacked to mainlaind Europe via Jutland, it is still Scandinavian as the Vikings and Norse cultures came from there).


Norway is the second largest in land area but smallest in population. It takes up the western half pf the Scandinavian Peninsula.


All three countries have history spanning back to the Viking Age, when their people, called Vikings (also know as Norsemen) led expeditions of war, raids, trade, and exchange to other places in Europe and other continents.


The three countries have a long history of conflict, exchange, and infleunce.
